Password Security


Passwords in the internet world are extremely common. Most websites require a person to sign up to an account. Whether this is a social media account or clothing website. To create an account a password is required. This password allows only the user to access the account.



Having a secure password is essential for staying safe online and keeping personal information protected. Hackers who intend on using your accounts to commit crimes will try and obtain your password to gain access. About 80% of data breaches in 2019 were caused by password compromise. If they succeed this could then lead on to crimes such as identity theft. The hacker could also use your accounts to gain paymeny information from you. Then commit further crimes with your details.

The best way to stay safe online is by creating a password that is secure. Let us start with common mistakes when creating a password and what not to include.

Numbers

Avoid using the default passwords such as ‘1234’. Use a range of numbers (but not significant dates, I.E birthday). 4 of the top 5 most common passwords in the world in 2021 are just numbers, meaning it will be extremely easy to guess. These examples include: "123456", "123456789" and "12345".

Characters

If allowed use special characters, as this will increase the time for hackers to get into your account. Similarly, use plenty of characters, as the longer the password, the more time it'll take to guess. A long password is more secure than a short password.

Uniqueness

A password should be unique for each account. It should not be shared over multiple sites. In addition to this, don't use common dictionary words, as dictionaries are used by hackers to guess your passwords.

It can be tempting to create a password using memorable information. Like personal details. Although easy to remember these words/ numbers make it easy for hackers to correctly guess your password and access your accounts.

If your password is not secure, we highly suggest creating a new one. Some key characteristics for a good password include:

  • A range of lower- and upper-case letters.

  • A range of numbers (but not significant dates. I.E birthday.)

  • Special characters and plenty of characters. A long password is more secure than a short password.

  • Different passwords for each website/ app.
