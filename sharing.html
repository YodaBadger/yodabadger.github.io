Sharing Information

Sharing Information Online


The problem with Sharing details Online In the vast world of the internet, it can be tempting to just share information with others without thinking twice about any consequences. It may not occur to somebody that sharing information about their life could be unsafe. However, sharing too much information, or the wrong kind of information, could lead to a person being hacked. Hackers can use the information shared to guess a person's passwords. Identity thieves could also steal a person's information to commit fraud.

Social Media


Social media can be a wonderful place to share information with family and friends. However, if the correct security measures are not put in place that information can be seen by anybody. Hackers can use information gained from social media platforms to aid them in their illegal hacking activities.

Comment Sections


A lot of websites on the internet have comments sections. Anyone can post a comment, either anonymously, or by logging in and creating an account. Hackers are smart and can gather information on a person by tracking the comments they leave on websites. This information gathering could aid them in getting information to hack that person. Similarly, chat forums and online chat websites are another place where hackers could be lurking, waiting to target a victim and steal information.

How to Share Information Safely

Catfishing

Be careful when sharing information with strangers online. Only share personal information when you know for definite that the person you are sharing with is someone you know and trust. Catfishing is a term used when someone pretends to be someone else online. Make sure you do not fall for a catfish and share information that you would not feel comfortable sharing with a stranger.

Keep Security Settings
Up-to-Date

Make sure that you keep an eye on your security settings on websites and social media sights. Updates to social media sites sometimes enable more people than you intended too to see the content you are posting. So, keep an eye on your settings and who can see your posts.

Keep Private Information Offline

Never share personal information online, such as your home address and passwords. These should never be posted online or sent in an email. Be cautious about sharing big life events or full names on public websites where anyone can see the information. Fraudsters can take that information to commit their crime. Remember that what is put online stays online. So, think carefully before posting anything.

Additional Tips


Don't Share Information over Email

Avoid sending personal and sensitive information over email. Emails accounts can be hacked and are not always secure.

Keep Your Connection Secure

Make sure you always use a secure connection when sending files. An unsecure connection opens your files to be snatched by hackers.

Keep Confidential Information Secure

If possible, avoid the use of USB's to store important and private information as they are easily lost.

Watch out for Dodgy Links

Always double check links, especially if sent randomly through email, to make sure the link is what it says it is. If in doubt, visit the website manually by typing the URL into your browser.
